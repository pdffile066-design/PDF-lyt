About the Flatten PDF Tool

What Does "Flatten PDF" Mean?

Flattening a PDF means taking all the separate elements of the document—like text, images, form fields, and annotations—and merging them into a single, static layer. Imagine gluing all the pictures and sticky notes onto a piece of paper; once it's done, you can't move them anymore. That's what flattening does for a PDF.

  • Security: It prevents others from editing form fields or removing your annotations and signatures.
  • Compatibility: A flattened PDF will look and print exactly the same across all devices and PDF viewers, avoiding issues with complex layers.
  • Finalizing Documents: It's the perfect final step before sharing a contract, a filled form, or an annotated document.
